About Blue & Fly

What’s really exciting about the little restaurant that opened last September is that it has a lot of dishes you won’t recognize, dishes that you’ve likely never had before. And that’s when things get fun.

Owner Yue “Joe” Cong told me after my three visits that he decided on that menu — a mix of familiar and unfamiliar — for very specific reasons. He knew, after working for a number of years at his father’s restaurant, Hunan Garden, that he wanted to focus on what he calls “authentic” Chinese food, dishes he grew up eating in China before he moved here at age 21, and dishes he’d eaten outside of Omaha, in cities such as New York and Chicago.

If you couldn’t tell, I really like Blue & Fly. I’m thrilled that Cong decided to open his restaurant closer to midtown, because until now, all my favorite Chinese spots — including Canton House and New Gold Mountain — have been west of 72nd Street. Midtowners and downtowners, along with the rest of the city, take note: There’s a food experience waiting to be had. Get there. (Author: Sarah Baker Hannsen)
